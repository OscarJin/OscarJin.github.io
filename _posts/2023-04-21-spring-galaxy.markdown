Spring in March and April is really the best season for stargazing! In just one night, you can see the winter Milky Way, the spring galaxy arch bridge, and the summer galaxy center all at once.

The winter Milky Way rises in the first half of the night and sets at around midnight, then the summer Milky Way rises and reaches a height of 30-40 degrees at around 3 or 4 am. 

At the end of March, my friends and I made a surprise visit to Inner Mongolia to photograph the Milky Way. There are many lonely trees in the grasslands of Inner Mongolia, so if you look carefully, you can find a dead tree in good shape as a landscape. In the second half of the night, as the summer Milky Way gradually rises, the silver heart straddles the lone tree, and only words like eternal, peaceful, and lonely can describe this scene.
