This article will exchange some of my personal views on the blockchain. Of course, if you have a basic understanding of it, you may understand it better.

Check out the blockchain on [Wikipedia][blockchain-wiki] for more technical info.

I came up with the following ideas when I was carrying out the project, Blockchain-Based E-Document Trading System (view the repository on [GitHub][github]). After having discussed them with my tutor, I finished the following article.

Decentralization is the main character of a blockchain, which is also a breakthrough in human’s thoughts, representing what those who pursue freedom long for. 

However, the society that we live in is actually centralized. We have centralized state apparatus. In our country, particularly, the trust system has formed a strict social hierarchy, from the government’s central bank to the four state-owned commercial banks, namely COB, CCB, ABC and ICBC, and finally, to hundreds of thousands of small private banks. Therefore, we must obey the values of the teams or organizations that control the core of the society. 

In business, the obedience represents an inequal relationship between the provider and the receiver, but the decentralization can help. Though it needs to be pointed out that such an innovation would be suppressed by the centralized, the blockchain is now being studied in computer science and some fundamental issues have not been engaged in yet, so it is in a more subtle stage.

The blockchain technology is not mature at the moment. Though Bitcoin has no function except being used as tokens, its unique pricing and trading system is accepted by all the users. Being accepted is what makes Bitcoin worthy, instead of the basic technology to support it. Bitcoin is backed by a large group of people, and this is the so-called credit. Credit can't be seen or touched, but it is naturally formed. Why we believe in the central bank? Because all of us do so, and this is the bedrock of the its credit, which, however, is driven by the state apparatus.

Technically, decentralization is not actually reliable. What is most criticized is that bitcoin mining is ultimately controlled by several major mines, with mining machines increasingly concentrated. If most of the world's mining machines are gathered in a particular individual, Bitcoin can mostly be dug out merely by him. Then he will have a lot of Bitcoin in his hand. When he has more than 50% of the Bitcoin in his hand, although the blockchain technology determines that the tokens are decentralized, it is actually centralized because the price of Bitcoin is controlled by a few people.

Therefore, the blockchain is worth exploring not only in the aspect of technology itself, but also in the aspect of social factors including morality and laws.

Perhaps the opinions are quite subjective and slightly negative, any comments are welcomed!
